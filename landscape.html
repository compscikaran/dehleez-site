"I believe in God, only I spell it nature."
Frank Lloyd Wright

Nature is as much about light and wind as about rocks and trees. Creating situations so one can remain in commune with the changing rhythms of nature even when one lives in the concrete jungle called a city, where one can converse with its myriad forms is what we aspire to provide for our clients.

Muted light is the essence of design in the Indian context. This tonality is achieved through three dimensional parasols, elements as pergolas and even through the structure. a simple intervention such as appropriate orientation is sufficient to facilitate natural ventilation and enable efficacy of energy consumption. The flora is selected to suit the microclimate and be visually pleasing. The employment of 'left-over' stones in the hard landscape is ironically an oxymoron of killing two birds with the same stone, for it embellishes the aesthetics and reaffirms our commitment to recycling and reuse of resources, indeed the need of the hour.
